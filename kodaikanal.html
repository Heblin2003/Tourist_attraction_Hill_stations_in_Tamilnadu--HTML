KODAIKANAL:Kodaikanal, often referred to as the "Princess of 
Hill Stations," is a charming hill station located in the Dindigul
district of Tamil Nadu, India.Perched at an altitude of around
2,133 meters in the Palani Hills of the Western Ghats, Kodaikanal
is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, mist-covered valleys, 
dense forests, and enchanting waterfalls. The town offers a serene
and tranquil atmosphere, making it a popular destination for 
nature lovers, honeymooners, and those seeking a peaceful retreat.

TOP ATTRACTION IN KODAIKANAL

The star-shaped Kodai Lake is a centerpiece of Kodaikanal.The outflow from the Kodai Lake has given birth to a 180-feet tall waterfall called Silver Cascade,one of the top places to visit in Kodaikanal.

Chettiar park is located near the Kurinji Andavar Temple, Chettiar Park is a well-maintained park with manicured lawns, colorful flowers, and beautiful sculptures. It's an ideal place for a leisurely stroll or a picnic amidst.

The Pine Forest in Kodaikanal is a scenic spot featuring tall pine trees, creating a serene and tranquil atmosphere. Taking a walk through the forest is a refreshing experience, and it offers excellent photo opportunities.

Pay a visit to the Kurinji Andavar Temple, dedicated to Lord Murugan. The temple is famous for its association with the blooming of the Kurinji flowers that occur once in every twelve years, painting the hills in vibrant blue hues.

Explore the Shembaganur Museum, also known as the Kodaikanal Museum, which showcases an extensive collection of preserved flora and fauna, fossils, rocks, and artifacts related to the region's natural history.

Visit the KodaikanalSolar Observatory , operated by the Indian Institute of Astrophysics. It houses various astronomical instruments and provides a fascinating insight into solar research and observation.

THINGS TO SEE IN KODAIKANAL

Take a stroll along Anna Salai, the bustling street market in Kodaikanal, and indulge in some shopping. You'll find a variety of handicrafts, souvenirs, clothing, spices, and homemade chocolates.

Located at an altitude of 6,600 feet, Dolphin's Nose is a unique rock formation that resembles the shape of a dolphin's nose. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and the Catherine Falls.

When it comes to food, Kodaikanal offers some delicious local delicacies. Don't miss trying the famous Kodaikanal homemade chocolates available in various flavors. Also, taste the local specialty called Poomparuppu, a sweet and savory dish made from tender coconut, jaggery, and lentils.

Also known as Devil's Kitchen, Guna Caves is an intriguing natural formation that attracts adventure seekers. The caves are surrounded by dense forests and have a mysterious ambiance, making them an exciting place to explore.

HOW? WHERE? WHEN?

The nearest airport to Kodaikanal is Madurai Airport, located about 120 kilometers away. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Kodaikanal.The best time to visit Kodaikanal is during the summer months of April to June when the weather is mild and pleasant. This is an ideal time for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

The nearest railway station is Kodai Road, which is well-connected to major cities. From Kodai Road, you can take a taxi or bus to reach Kodaikanal.Monsoon season from July to September brings moderate rainfall, which enhances the greenery but can also result in slippery roads and limited outdoor activities.

Kodaikanal is well-connected by roadways, and regular bus services operate from cities like Chennai, Bangalore, and Madurai. You can also choose to drive to Kodaikanal or hire a taxi.Winter, from October to February, offers cool temperatures, making it a pleasant time to visit. However, the evenings can get chilly, so carrying warm clothing is advisable.
