YELAGIRI:In Tamil Nadu’s Vellore district, Yelagiri is a little hill
town that is part of the eastern kutch. It’s the ideal location for 
a short city vacation. Visitors can unwind and savour the 
tranquillity that the mountain village has to grant. Yelagiri is 
well known for rock climbing despite being a mountain range. You 
can purchase homemade nectar or organic honey produced by bee 
populations in the wild environment in Yelagiri, which is renowned
as the top honey exporter in Tamil Nadu.

TOP ATTRACTION IN YELAGIRI

The Punganoor Lake is one of the most popular artificial lakes in Tamil nadu. The man made lake covers nearly 60 sq. meters of land. There is also a fountain in the middle and boating facilities are available.

Jalagamparai Waterfall is a popular natural attraction in yelagiri The river stream of Attaaru, flowing through the valleys of Yelagiri Hills, creates this magnificent cascade at an approximate distance of 37 km from Yelagiri Town.

Nature Park is located adjacent to Punganoor Lake, spread over an area of 12 acres. Several species of plants suited to the rocky terrain of the region have been planted in the park. The park also has an artificial waterfall.

The Swamimalai hill offers a breathtaking view from the summit. You can see the complete valley below you as well as the villages of Yelagiri.A cool breeze with the spectacular view makes you feel ecstatic and relaxing.

Velavan Temple is dedicated to worshipping Lord Murugan,known for its religious significance. The temple has a fantastic view of the surrounding hills and it houses a rare Gadothgajan, the son of Bheema, at its entrance.

Yelagiri Forest hill is a hillside place with beautiful scenic hiking trails and marked temples.The hilly terrain of Yelagiri Forest hills spreads across an area of 30 square kilometres ,an altitude of 1110.6 metres above the sea level.

Vainnu Bappu Observatory (VBO) is located at Kavalur, near Yelagiri, in the state of Tamil Nadu. This observatory was established in the 1970s, at Kavalur, by M.K. Vainnu Bappu, the former director of the Kodaikanal observatory.

Government Herbal Farm in Yelagiri is a must visit for all nature lovers. The flora and medicinal plants around this farm are a sight to behold.This farm has many other places to visit like an aquarium, musical fountain etc.

Fundera park is an iconic place in Yelagiri that is a paradise for bird watchers and nature enthusiasts. This is an aviary and a petting farm where you can find more than 3000 species of exotic birds.

The Nilavoor lake is a beautiful artificial with turquoise waters and green surroundings. It is becoming a popular summer retreat for travellers in Tamil Nadu. The lake is a most preferred for boating and nature walk.

A popular destination for adventure enthusiasts, the Yelagiri Adventure Camp offers a range of thrilling activities such as rock climbing,trekking,and camping. It's an ideal spot for those seeking an outdoor experience

Cloud Forest Entertainment Park is one of the popular attractions in this area, offering a range of thrilling experiences for visitors of all ages. This park includes a range of activities such as zip lining, ropes courses, obstacle courses, etc.

Amirthi Falls is located in the midst of Amirthi Zoological Park, in Vellore district of Tamil Nadu. The cascading clear water flows through Amrithi forests, deep ravines, and beautiful hills.One has to trek 1.5 km from the point of entry to waterfall to witness the majestic view.

A vast territory enveloped in a stretch of varied tropics and exotic flowers, the SRL Rose garden is well maintained and most importantly, the number of flowers this area holds is absolutely fascinating.he garden also coincides with a number of waterfalls that make the place a delight

The cuisine of Yelagiri comprised of traditional South Indian cuisine mainly rice based meals. Cooked vegetables garnished with coconut are eaten with rice. Jackfruit is the specialty of the region. The popular sweet dishes of Yelagiri include paysam and jangiri.

the Perumal Temple is one of the biggest temples in all of Yelagiri. A truly holy place, the temple is carved between rocks up a hill from where the landscapes look amusingly impressive. Among the many places to visit in Yelagiri, a visit to Perumal temple is highly recommended,

HOW? WHERE? WHEN?

By Air: The nearest airport is Bengaluru International Airport, which is approximately 170 kilometers away from Yelagiri. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Yelagiri.It's advisable to avoid visiting Yelagiri during the summer months (April to June) as the temperatures can soar, making it less favorable for outdoor exploration.

The nearest railway station to Yelagiri is Jolarpettai Junction, which is around 20 kilometers away. Regular trains connect Jolarpettai with major cities in India. From the railway station, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Yelagiri. The best time to visit Yelagiri is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The temperatures during this time range from 15 to 25 degrees Celsius, making it comfortable for sightseeing and exploring the attractions.

Yelagiri is well-connected by road and can be reached by buses or private vehicles. It is approximately 230 kilometers away from Bengaluru and around 160 kilometers away from Chennai. Yelagiri offers a range of accommodation options, including resorts, hotels, guesthouses, and homestays. You can choose from various budget and luxury options based on your preferences.
